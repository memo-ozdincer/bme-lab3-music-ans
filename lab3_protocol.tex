\section{Hypothesis}

\textit{If a subject performs pushups with progressively wider hand placement, then the relative EMG activation of the pectoralis major will increase while the triceps brachii activation decreases, because a wider hand position lengthens the moment arm about the shoulder and shortens it about the elbow.  Therefore, hand width systematically shifts the mechanical demand---and thus motor unit recruitment---between the two primary pushup movers.}

% ----------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Background \& Rationale}

A pushup is a closed kinetic chain exercise involving simultaneous elbow extension and shoulder horizontal adduction.  The two dominant muscles are:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Triceps brachii} --- the primary elbow extensor.  When the hands are \emph{narrow} (closer together), the elbow must flex more deeply and the moment arm about the elbow joint increases, demanding greater triceps force.
    \item \textbf{Pectoralis major} --- the primary shoulder horizontal adductor.  When the hands are \emph{wide}, the shoulder is more abducted and the horizontal adduction moment arm increases, demanding greater pectoral force.
\end{itemize}

By recording surface EMG from both muscles during narrow, standard, and wide pushups, we expect to observe a \textbf{crossover pattern}: triceps activation highest during narrow pushups, pectoralis activation highest during wide pushups, with standard pushups showing balanced recruitment.

This effect is well-documented in exercise science (Cogley et al., 2005; Gouvali \& Boudolos, 2005) and provides a clear, measurable, and physiologically grounded result.

\section{Hand Width Definitions}

Before placing electrodes, \textbf{measure the subject's biacromial shoulder width} (distance between the two bony tips of the shoulders---the acromions) with the measuring tape.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{My shoulder width:} \underline{\hspace{3cm}} cm
\vspace{0.2cm}

The three hand positions are defined as the distance between the \emph{centres of the palms} when in pushup position:

\begin{center}

\begin{tabular}{@{} l c p{6cm} @{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Width} & \textbf{Hand spacing} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
Narrow   & $0.5 \times$ shoulder width & Hands close together, roughly beneath the centre of the chest.  Thumbs may nearly touch. \\

\section{BioRadio \& Electrode Setup}

\subsection{BioCapture Configuration}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item Open BioCapture.  Turn the BioRadio ON.
    \item \textbf{Device $\rightarrow$ Select Device $\rightarrow$} your BioRadio $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Connect}.
    \item Click \textbf{Device Config}:
    \begin{itemize}[nosep]
        \item Check \textbf{CH1}.  Name: \textbf{``Triceps''}.  Type: \textbf{EMG}.
        \item Check \textbf{CH2}.  Name: \textbf{``Pectoralis''}.  Type: \textbf{EMG}.
        \item Uncheck other channels.
        \item Sampling rate: \textbf{2\,kHz}.
    \end{itemize}
    \item Click \textbf{Program Device}.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Electrode Placement}

All electrodes go on the \textbf{same side} of the body (dominant arm side, usually right).  Use the subject's dominant arm because it typically has better motor control and produces cleaner signals.

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.4}
\begin{tabular}{@{} l l p{7.5cm} @{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Channel} & \textbf{Muscle} & \textbf{Placement} \\
\midrule
CH1 (+/--) & Triceps brachii (lateral head) & Two electrodes on the \textbf{back of the upper arm}, midway between the shoulder (posterior deltoid fold) and the elbow (olecranon).  Place them $\sim$3\,cm apart, \textbf{along} the length of the arm (parallel to the muscle fibres). \\
\addlinespace
CH2 (+/--) & Pectoralis major (sternal portion) & Two electrodes on the \textbf{chest}, approximately 2 finger-widths below the collarbone, at the midpoint between the sternum and the anterior axillary fold (armpit crease).  Place them $\sim$3\,cm apart, roughly \textbf{horizontal} (along the fibre direction). \\
\addlinespace
GND & --- & One electrode on the \textbf{acromion} (bony tip of the shoulder on the same side).  This is between the two measurement sites, minimising common-mode noise. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Skin preparation:}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item Clean each electrode site with an alcohol wipe.  Let dry 10\,s.
    \item For best adhesion during pushups, firmly press down the edges of each electrode.
    \item If the subject has significant body hair at the electrode sites, shaving may improve signal quality (optional, ask the subject first).
\end{itemize}

\begin{warningbox}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item Warm up before doing pushups (arm circles, light stretching) to prevent injury.
    \item If the subject feels pain in their wrists, shoulders, or chest, \textbf{stop immediately}.
    \item The narrow pushup width puts extra stress on the wrists.  If uncomfortable, widen slightly.
    \item Do \textbf{not} do pushups to failure.  5 controlled reps per set is sufficient.
\end{itemize}
\end{warningbox}

\subsection{Signal Check}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item Click \textbf{Run} in BioCapture.  Set Graph Timespan to \textbf{10\,s}.
    \item Right-click y-axis $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Y-Axis $\rightarrow$ Auto Scale Quick} on both channels.
    \item \textbf{Test CH1 (Triceps):} extend your arm straight behind you (like a tricep kickback) --- CH1 should spike.
    \item \textbf{Test CH2 (Pec):} press your palms together in front of your chest --- CH2 should spike.
    \item If a channel is flat or railed: re-check electrode connections, skin prep, and ground.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Experimental Protocol}

\textbf{Total duration: $\sim$7--8 minutes of recording.}

\subsection{Pre-experiment Checklist}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item[$\square$] Shoulder width measured, hand positions marked with tape on the floor.
    \item[$\square$] BioRadio connected, channels configured, signals verified.
    \item[$\square$] BioRadio clipped to subject's waistband or placed near pushup area.
    \item[$\square$] Electrode edges pressed down firmly (they'll peel during pushups if loose).
    \item[$\square$] Subject has warmed up (arm circles, a few practice pushups).
    \item[$\square$] Stopwatch ready.  Printed protocol + pen in hand.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Recording Procedure}

The \textbf{partner} operates BioCapture, the stopwatch, and calls out instructions.  The \textbf{subject} does the pushups.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Start recording:} Click \textbf{Record} (red dot) in BioCapture.  Save as \texttt{pushup\_emg}.  Start the stopwatch simultaneously.
\end{enumerate}

\toprule
\textbf{\#} & \textbf{Condition} & \textbf{Duration} & \textbf{Stopwatch} & \textbf{Instructions} \\
\midrule
\rowcolor{gray!10}

\rowcolor{red!8}

\begin{tipbox}
\textbf{Tempo control:} Have the partner count out loud:  ``Down-two-three-four, Up-two-three-four'' for each rep.  Consistent tempo is important so that differences in EMG are due to hand width, not speed.
\end{tipbox}

\subsection{Pushup Form}

Consistent form is critical---differences in form create differences in EMG that confound the hand-width effect.

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Body position:} Straight line from head to heels.  No sagging hips or piking.
    \item \textbf{Depth:} Lower until the upper arm is \textbf{parallel to the floor} (roughly 90$^\circ$ elbow flexion).  Do not go deeper for narrow pushups just because it's possible---match depth across all widths.
    \item \textbf{Speed:} 4 seconds per rep (2\,s eccentric / 2\,s concentric).  Partner counts tempo.
    \item \textbf{Breathing:} Inhale on the way down, exhale on the way up.  Do not hold your breath.
    \item \textbf{If the subject cannot complete 5 reps at a given width:} Do as many as possible with good form.  Note the actual number in the log.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Setup}
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item Install: \texttt{pip3 install numpy scipy matplotlib pandas}
    \item Open \texttt{analyze\_emg.py}.  Update \texttt{PHASE\_START\_S} with your actual timestamps.

\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Output Figures}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{fig1\_emg\_timeseries.png} --- RMS envelope of both channels over the full recording.  You should see clear bursts during each pushup set with distinct amplitude differences.
    \item \textbf{fig2\_emg\_comparison.png} --- Bar charts of mean RMS per condition for each muscle, including baseline for reference.
    \item \textbf{fig3\_grouped\_comparison.png} --- \textbf{Key figure:} grouped bar chart showing triceps vs.\ pectoralis side-by-side at each width.  This is where the crossover pattern should be visible.
    \item \textbf{fig4\_emg\_snippets.png} --- 2-second raw EMG windows from each width, showing waveform amplitude differences.
    \item \textbf{fig5\_peak\_comparison.png} --- Peak RMS during each set.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Signal Processing}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Bandpass filter:} 20--450\,Hz (4th-order Butterworth).  Removes motion artifact (low freq) and high-frequency noise.
    \item \textbf{RMS envelope:} 250\,ms sliding window.  Proportional to the number and firing rate of active motor units.
    \item \textbf{Mean RMS:} Average over the entire pushup set.  Reflects overall muscle demand.
    \item \textbf{Peak RMS:} Maximum RMS in the set.  Reflects peak effort (usually during the concentric ``push'' phase).
\end{itemize}
